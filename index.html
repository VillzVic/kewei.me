Research Experience

  • An evolutionary algorithm for timetabling problem
    2014-present

    Supervisor: Jean-Paul Boufflet, Aziz Moukrim
    Laboratory Heudiasyc, Compiègne, France

    This is the current research I'm working with, at this time, we investigated a memetic approach to the instance of our university and all instances from ITC2007. We defined chromosome in our problem and the associated encoding and decoding methods in order to convert a solution into a chromosome and vice versa, and we have integrated three heuristic solvers into the memetic in order to promote the population of chromosome. In August, I attended the PATAT2014 in the United Kingdom to present our work and an article is published on the proceeding of the conference: Exam timetabling at Université de Technologie de Compiégne: a memetic approach. (details).

    Keywords: Genetic Algorithm, Meta-heuristic, Chromosome

  • A heuristic approaches for education timetabling
    2013-2014

    Supervisor: Jean-Paul Boufflet, Aziz Moukrim
    Laboratory Heudiasyc, Compiègne, France

    During the research, we defined a evaluation function to calculate the cost of the current solution and I implemented a Deconstructive and Constructive(DC) solver as a local search method to improve the solution and after applying the DC, the quality of our solution is generally better than the solution obtained by IP method especially comparing to the calculation time. We also injected the solution of heuristic solver into the previous IP model and normally the IP solver can continue improving the solution. In order to test our algorithm, I also created an instance generator which use student enrollment record of our university to the instance file that can be used directly by our solver. And I also built some tools in Python to visualize the solution graphically so that the practitioner can judge the solution more efficiently.

    Keywords: Heuristic, Local Search, Instance Generator

  • Preprocessing and resolution of examination timetabling problem by liner programming
    2013

    Supervisor: Jean-Paul Boufflet, Aziz Moukrim
    Laboratory Heudiasyc, Compiègne, France

    In this research, we used integer programming to build a mathematical model for solving the problem of exam timetabling of UTC, in the model, we introduced some new hard and soft constraint besides that in the ITC2007, and these constraints meet the organization requirements of our university and give more flexibility to the students.
    In order to solve the model more efficiently, we introduced many preprocessing methods to reduce the model size, for instance, we introduced some incompatibilities of supervisor and room capacity and we also pre-calculated the lower bound for some evaluation parameters in order to know the distance between the optimum solution and the current solution.

    Keywords: Integer Programming, Lower bound, CPLEX

  • A new system to measure the wind speed
    2008-2009

    Supervisor: ZHANG Tianru
    Zhuzhou N°2 middle school, Zhuzhou, China

    This is my first scientific experience, the work was under the supervision of Mr. Zhang, who is a very famous teacher in youth creativity and innovation education. When I proposed my ideal of creating a measuring device for wind, his gave me a lot of instructions and introduced many experts from both research institution and university. I went to the local weather station several times to test our device and adapted it into the current system of the station under his help.
    The device can measure the wind in real-time and send back result in wireless way, and the dedicated software can read the data and present it directly on the screen which include the current wind speed, 2-hour mean speed, 24-hour mean speed, wind change graph etc.
    My invention was selected by the China Soong Ching Ling Foundation, and I got a silver prize for my invention, later, I wrote an article about my invention and this article won the third prize for the awarding program for Future scientists which is organized by Chinese Academy of Sciences.
    This invention is under protection as an Utility model in China (details).

    Keywords: Wind measuring, Future Scientist, Invention
